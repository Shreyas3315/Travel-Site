London

London is the capital and largest city of England and the United Kingdom, with a population of just over 9 million. It stands on the River Thames in south-east England.As one of the world's major global cities London exerts a strong influence on its arts, entertainment, fashion, commerce and finance, education, health care, media, science and technology, tourism, and transport and communications.Its GDP (€801.66 billion in 2017) makes it the largest urban economy in Europe,and it is one of the major financial centres in the world.





Rome

Rome is the capital city of Italy. It is also the capital of the Lazio region, the centre of the Metropolitan City of Rome, and a special comune named Comune di Roma Capitale. With 2,860,009 residents in 1,285 km2 (496.1 sq mi), Rome is the country's most populated comune and the third most populous city in the European Union by population within city limits. Rome is generally considered to be the "cradle of Western civilization and Christian culture", and the centre of the Catholic Church.

Cluj-Napoca

Cluj-Napoca is the fourth-most populous city in Romania.It is the seat of Cluj County in the northwestern part of the country.The city is one of the most important academic, cultural, industrial and business centres in Romania. Among other institutions, it hosts the country's largest university, Babeș-Bolyai University, with its botanical garden; nationally renowned cultural institutions; as well as the largest Romanian-owned commercial bank.

Paris

Paris is the capital and most populous city of France and has been one of the world's major centres of finance, diplomacy, commerce, fashion, gastronomy, science, and arts, and has sometimes been referred to as the capital of the world or "the City of Light".Paris is a major railway, highway, and air-transport hub served by two international airports: Paris–Charles de Gaulle and Paris–Orly.

Amsterdam

Amsterdam is the capital and most populous city of the Netherlands.Originating as a small fishing village in the late 12th century, Amsterdam became one of the most important ports in the world during the Dutch Golden Age of the 17th century, and became the leading centre for the finance and trade sectors.The city is also well known for its nightlife and festival activity.

Barcelona

Barcelona is a city on the coast of northeastern Spain.The city is home to two of the most prestigious universities in Spain. The headquarters of the Union for the Mediterranean are located in Barcelona. The city is known for hosting the 1992 Summer Olympics as well as world-class conferences and expositions and also many international sport tournaments.
